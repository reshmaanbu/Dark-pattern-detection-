Learn About Dark Patterns

Understand the deceptive tactics used against you online — and how to spot them.

⏰

Fake Urgency

Urgency patterns create artificial time pressure to prevent you from thinking carefully about a purchase or decision. They work by triggering your fear of missing out (FOMO).

Examples:

  • "Offer expires in 10 minutes!"
  • "Act now — limited time deal"
  • "Today only — don't miss out"
💡 Tip: Always wait a day before making a big decision. The "offer" will usually still be there.
📦

False Scarcity

Scarcity patterns exaggerate how few items are left or how high demand is, pushing you to buy before you're ready. Often these numbers are fake or automatically reset.

Examples:

  • "Only 3 left in stock!"
  • "Selling fast — limited availability"
  • "While supplies last"
💡 Tip: Check another retailer. If they have plenty of stock, the scarcity was manufactured.
👥

Social Pressure

These patterns use crowd behavior to make you feel like everyone else is buying, so you should too. The numbers shown are often exaggerated or fabricated.

Examples:

  • "47 people are viewing this right now"
  • "Best seller — join 10,000+ customers"
  • "Trending — most popular choice"
💡 Tip: Popularity ≠ quality. Make decisions based on your own needs, not crowd behavior.
😔

Confirmshaming

This pattern frames your refusal of an offer in a self-deprecating or negative way — making you feel foolish, cheap, or incompetent for saying no.

Examples:

  • "No thanks, I hate saving money"
  • "I prefer to pay full price"
  • "No thanks, I don't want to be smarter"
💡 Tip: It's always okay to say no. The language is designed to make you feel bad — don't let it.
